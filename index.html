Switzerland
Switzerland boasts an extraordinary variety of landscapes and habitats, ranging from the Central Plateau to the high peaks of the Alps. Its abundant natural ecosystems like the Alps, wetlands and forests are in close proximity to urban centres and are a particular feature of the Swiss landscape.
Finland
Finland is Europe's most forested country. About 70% of the land is covered with trees. Most forests are coniferous, as the country lies at the western edge of the coniferous taiga forest zone that stretched off eastwards through Russia and Siberia.
Iceland
Icelandic nature offers an incredible variety of landscapes – from fjords, to mountains, to glaciers, to lava fields – in a small country. What's more, the air is pristine and the water crystal clear.
Australia
The Illawarra Flame Tree is renowned for its bright red bell-shaped flowers that look like a bonfire when the tree is leafless. It's a deciduous tree native to the eastern parts of Australia. It offers a brilliant contrast when planted near a Jacaranda.
Netherland
The national parks of the Northern Netherlands offer an enchanting variety of natural splendour. Dense forests and vast heathlands in the Drents-Friese Wold are home to rare animals. Lauwersmeer's dynamic ecosystems unite fresh and salt water.
Ireland
Ireland is known for its amazing cliffs. Top of the list is Slieve League on Donegal's west coast; it's one of the highest marine cliffs in Europe. The straight 985-foot drop from Slieve League into the crashing Atlantic below is sure to get your heart racing.
